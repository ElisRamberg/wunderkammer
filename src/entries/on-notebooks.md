The hardest part of keeping a notebook is the temptation to wait until you have
something worthy of it. So you wait, and the page stays blank, and the thought
you had on Tuesday is gone by Thursday.

The fix is to lower the bar until it disappears. A note can be one sentence. A
prototype can be ugly. An idea can be wrong. The only requirement is that it
*exists somewhere you'll see it again*.

> The point of a notebook is not to hold finished thoughts. It's to hold thoughts
> long enough that they can become finished.

That's the whole philosophy here: capture first, polish never or later. The
status badge does the honest work of saying "this is still an idea" so you don't
have to feel like everything you publish is a claim.
